1960's-1970's

1961

Packet-Switching

Leonard Kleinrock of MIT pioneers the packet-switching concept in his doctoral thesis.

1963

ASCII is Developed

ASCII (American Standard Code for Information Exchange) was the first universal standard for computers that permitted machines built by different manufacturers to exchange data.

1967

First Real-Time Flight Visual Flight Simulator

Developed by Danny Cohen, he also developed the first real-time radar simulator. His work led to the development of the Cohen-Sutherland computer graphics line clipping algorithms, which he created with Ivan Sutherland.

1969

ARPANET

Established in 1969, ARPANET was used by the military to make communication easier. Using four computers in different areas and linked together, they were able to send information between each. other. It was the foundation of the internet.
